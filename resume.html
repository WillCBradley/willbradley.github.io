1. We'll start with the most interesting stuff first...

After scoring highly in the UCAT (Australia's MCAT), I was invited to work for MedEntry as a question writer. I did this for 15 months, before noticing an interesting opportunity.

At the time, MedEntry's newsletter was structured as a series of repackaged blog posts. Extremely useful, but not extremely engaging. So I did the following:


Step 1: Bought a handful of copywriting books (see recommended reading).

Step 2: Wrote ~100,000 words of my own newsletters based on things I was learning (the latest of which you can find here).

Step 3: Took on a few free clients (here are their testimonials).

Step 4: Wrote a few sample emails upfront, and pitched an idea to a contact at MedEntry alongside my next submission of questions.


I was given a meeting with the company's Director of Education, and he liked the idea.

Since then, I've worked with MedEntry (and a company they invest in) on a range of projects, including newsletters, VSL scripts, and email series.

In the process, I'm learning how to work well in a team, write SOPs, and structure meetings (so that they're actually an efficient use of everybody's time!)

I never learned any of these skills at school, so this has been incredibly valuable...

But I also understand that a story like this is very uncommon, and therefore difficult to quantify or compare with others. Which leads us to the next section:
