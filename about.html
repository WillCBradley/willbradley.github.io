About

In early med school, it felt like the walls were closing in.

I could see AI getting smarter, but I was too busy memorising stuff to prepare.

Medicine seemed overpriced, like a bunch of overachievers racing to buy houses right before the 2008 crash...

And worst of all? I wasn't smart enough to find a way out.

So I started learning.

I began studying economics, and philosophy, and physics, and psychology, and business.

I picked up ideas from Charlie Munger, Nassim Taleb, Jeff Bezos, Epictetus, Adam Smith, Naval Ravikant, Richard Feynman, and countless others.

But I'm nowhere near finished. I'm trying to build a durable, practical set of ideas, and it'll probably take decades.

Here, you'll find a distillation of what I learn.
